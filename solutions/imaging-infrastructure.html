Why Most Imaging AI Fails at Deployment

Diagnostic AI performance is determined less by model architecture and more by the reliability of its training data.

Most imaging datasets contain hidden annotation inconsistencies, untracked revisions, and subgroup representation gaps. These weaknesses introduce calibration instability and unpredictable performance when models are deployed across new hospitals, scanner types, and patient populations.

Diagnostic labs and AI teams often discover these limitations late in development — slowing deployment, reducing institutional trust, and increasing validation burden.

To deploy safely and scale successfully, diagnostic AI systems require infrastructure that ensures the integrity and reliability of their training data.
