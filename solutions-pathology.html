Who it’s for
Pathology labs and pathology AI teams working with whole slide images (WSI).
WSI introduces unique variability: staining differences, scanning artifacts, and complex annotations. Trinzz brings structure, QC rigor, and traceability.
Common risks
Staining variability and scanner differences causing label inconsistency
Complex multi-region annotations with subjective boundaries
Insufficient consensus for ambiguous pathology patterns
Unclear dataset versions leading to irreproducible outcomes
Reliability controls we implement
WSI-specific protocols: staining variability handling and artifact guidelines
Multi-pass QC with disagreement resolution and concordance reporting
Structured sampling for rare patterns and hard negatives
Versioned releases with evidence packs per cohort and task

FAQ
Common questions from clinical and enterprise buyers.
How do you prove ground truth quality?

By pairing guidelines with measurable QC: sampling plans, reviewer accountability, concordance metrics, and exported QA reports.

Can we use our own clinicians?

Yes. Trinzz can onboard your experts into the workflow while enforcing the same governance, QC, and evidence outputs.

What if we’re already in production?

We start with monitoring: PSI/calibration thresholds, edge-case review queues, and a defined intervention workflow.
